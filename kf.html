Air India · Pilot Sector Report · Design Lead · 2024

Pilot App, a 0 to 1 project: from 15 minutes on paper to 2:30 on iPad.

Air India pilots filed flight, incident, and sector reports on paper: 10–15 minutes a shift, then 3–4 days before the data was re-keyed by hand. I designed the native iPadOS app that replaced it.

The Challenge

Tedious, error-prone paper forms, and a multi-day bottleneck on operational data the airline needed in real time.

My Role

Design lead. I interviewed pilots about the forms and the Airbus/Boeing reporting differences, then mapped database constraints with stakeholders. The unlock: sector reports didn't need to live inside the larger flight report.

The Approach

Field research with both Airbus and Boeing pilots, concept sketches reviewed before pixel design, and wireframes iterated through several rejected directions.

What Happened

Every tested pilot completed a submission unaided. One fix from testing: some text-only links read as icons, so pilots kept tapping the wrong thing, corrected in the next round.

The Challenge

Simplify a complex, high-stakes process. Early errors in a connection request caused disproportionate delays later, the desktop-only portal ignored on-site needs, and the system lacked data validation, so paperwork got lost and delays cascaded across every party involved.

Element Corp × TCS · Designer & Prototyper

Fleet Management Dashboard Design for Element Corp.

A modernized fleet management portal for Element Corp: linking drivers up with trucks and keeping their data, from driver's licenses to license plate numbers, visible to operators and the operations center.

~$300

$3M / yr
Projected across a 10,000-vehicle fleet

The Challenge

Operators and the operations center lacked clear visibility into which drivers were assigned to which vehicles, and driver records (licenses, plates, credentials) lived in a dated, desktop-bound dashboard that made everyday fleet tasks slow and error-prone.

My Role

Designer and prototyper. I designed the modernized portal and built the high-fidelity interactive prototypes in Axure that defined how it should work.

The Approach

Modernize the dashboard around the core task: linking and unlinking drivers and trucks. Drag-and-drop assignment made that fast and legible, driver data stayed attached to the right vehicle, and a responsive web layout took the tool beyond the desktop.

What I Delivered

A full interactive Axure prototype of the fleet portal: drag-and-drop driver–truck assignment, driver record management, and operator and operations center visibility, in a modern, responsive interface.

UX Wizards · Workshop Design & Facilitation · 2025

Bring Your Thing: a design review workshop I designed and led.

A structured peer-critique format for the UX Wizards community — designed from scratch to give designers real feedback on real work, not just moral support. The format scales from 8 to 50+ and has run multiple times across in-person and hybrid settings.

8K+
Community the workshop draws from
Multi-run
Format refined across multiple sessions
2025
Designed and facilitated solo
Fig. 01 — The workshop in action: structured critique rounds with rotating presenters and timed feedback.

The Problem

Design community events skew toward talks and demos. Designers rarely get structured, critical feedback on work-in-progress — the kind that actually makes the work better. I wanted to fix that.

My Role

Solo workshop designer and facilitator. I designed the format, the facilitation guide, the timing structure, and ran it — then iterated based on what I observed.

The Format

Participants bring a real design problem they're working on. Structured rounds with a presenter, a note-taker, and rotating critics. Timed feedback phases to prevent the usual domination by one voice. Warm-up exercises to get people into critique mode before the first case goes up.

What Worked

The timed structure was the unlock — it forced quieter participants to contribute and stopped the loudest person from monopolizing. Post-session feedback was consistently high; multiple attendees said it was the most useful UX Wizards event they'd been to.
